    Bis orat qui bene cantat !!

    Bis orat qui bene cantat

    Latin: Bis orat qui cantat has been traditionally attributed to St. Augustine (d. 430 CE). The version with the included “bene” is from the General Instruction of the Roman Missal and can be translated as “Whoever sings well prays twice over”. Hence singing, as a primary form of prayer, is a very ancient tradition in Christianity indeed!

    By the way, anyone can have problems with that “bene”! How could we be required to be great singers in order to pray twice? I had the question too, but soon I got it. It was my own perfectionism putting a slant on everything I hear and see. We are not asked to be professional operatic singers, but to sing well, with enthusiasm, and conviction, and joy. That is praying twice as well.

    Dusenge kabiri

    Frequently asked questions about Eastern Singers Group

    From the community, people would ask a question like; are you all from the Eastern Province Rwanda? or how can someone join your singing group? and more other questions regarding our notability.
    For the record, we have already answered to such ones and different more, and we're also open for further questions.

    Questions - Answers

    Are all ESG singers from the Eastern Province
    Answer
    No, ESG singers come from various regions across Rwanda, representing the diversity of our nation.
    How many are you in ESG
    Answer
    Now we are 39 singers in ESG.
    How can someone join your group
    Answer
    If you're interested in joining ESG, feel free to email us or reach out via WhatsApp using the provided contacts. We'll be happy to provide you with more information on the requirements and conditions.
    Is your singing group made by only youth people
    Answer
    Thankfully, no! The Eastern Singers Group welcomes individuals of all ages, including both young and adult members. Feel free to visit our about page for more information on the diverse members of ESG.
    Why Eastern Singers Group not "Eastern Singers Choir"
    Answer
    The decision to use 'ES Group' instead of 'ES Choir' stems from the fact that our members are involved in various other choirs. Additionally, logistical challenges prevent us from regularly gathering as a unified choir, as our members reside in different regions across the country.
    Where is Eastern Singers Group based
    Answer
    Currently, ESG primarily operates from the basement of Rutonde Parish, under the Archdiocese of Kigali. While we also have a presence at Kirehe Parish, Rutonde serves as our main location.
    How do you prepare for your activities and services
    Answer
    Initially, members engage in individual and choir rehearsals at both Rutonde and Kirehe Parishes. As the event approaches, we gather collectively at Rutonde Parish to finalize our preparations, whether it's for weddings, concerts, new song releases, or other occasions.
    Are all of you proficient in music, including sight-reading notes (solfeggio)
    Answer
    While not all of us excel in solfeggio, the majority, about eighty percent of male vocalists, are adept at reading notes, with a few female members also skilled. Nevertheless, we find it manageable to learn and even compose new music songs.
    How many original songs do you have
    Answer
    Currently, we have 18 songs that are used in Holy Mass, all of which are available on our website in music sheet format. Additionally, eight of these songs also have accompanying videos. Watch
